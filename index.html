Concerns about fat or cholesterol have caused some people to cut down on animal protein. Good news for meat lovers is that healthy options continue to increase. Dobbins: “Beef is leaner today than ever before, making it easier for Americans to include lean beef in a variety of healthy diet patterns. More than 65 percent of beef cuts available at the grocery store are considered ‘lean’ based on USDA standards. In addition, 17 of the top 25 most popular beef cuts sold are ‘lean.’” Kirchherr: “One of my favorite tips for efficient, informed shopping is to look for the word ‘loin’ or ‘round’ in the name to choose a leaner cut of meat. Pork tenderloin, for example, is an easy to cook, flavorful choice to make."
